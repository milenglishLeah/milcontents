Intermediate

파트 D: 최종 수정본 에세이

What Makes a Friendship Last

Helen Keller once said, "I would rather walk with a friend in the dark than alone in the light." People seek an abundance of friends and valuable friendships because these relationships are built on positivity, honesty, and trust. To build a resolute and stable friendship, one must practice these three essential qualities.

One way to cultivate a continuous friendship is by being positive. A positive attitude can uplift friends, while negativity can be destructive. For example, in *The Tale of Despereaux*, Roscuro initially loved light, but his friendship with the cynical Botticelli, who enjoyed causing others to suffer, slowly changed him. This shows how a friend's behavior can influence our own outlook. In contrast, a superior example of positive influence is Helen Keller. Though she was blind and deaf, she did not give up. She was distinct from others because she persevered and eventually graduated from college. This was a colossal triumph made possible by the support of her teacher and significant friend, Anne Sullivan. Therefore, having a positive mindset is essential for a lasting friendship.

Another way to maintain a stable friendship is by holding on to honesty. First, friends should not lie to each other. In the movie *Wonka*, Wonka’s companion was initially a foe, but they became friends. Wonka was always truthful because telling a lie offered no benefit. When Wonka was in enormous jeopardy near the end of the movie, his friend remembered Wonka's honest character and helped him escape danger. Second, it is important to believe in your friends. For example, in *Harry Potter*, Harry shows his affection and trust in Ron and Hermione by sharing his fears and facing gigantic challenges with them. In conclusion, to have an endless friendship, we must clasp on to honesty.

Lastly, to have an ongoing friendship, we must trust each other. First, trust helps to diminish conflicts. For example, in the TV show *Friends*, the six characters often had disagreements, but their arguments decreased when they began to trust each other more deeply. Second, friends should not spread gossip. I once had a friend who spread false information, and our conversations soon became monotonous and boring. Eventually, her other friends and I had to abandon her because of her meaningless talk. Fortunately, she stopped gossiping and we could rebuild our trust. In conclusion, trusting each other is fundamental to any strong friendship.

To build a stronger friendship, we should practice positive behavior, be honest, and trust each other. Being positive, holding on to honesty, and having trust will create meaningful development in a friendship. With these qualities, we can build high-quality friendships. If you want to have a constant and perpetual friendship, what will you do?

학생에게 보내는 편지

학생에게,

'오래가는 우정'에 대한 에세이 잘 읽었어요. 헬렌 켈러의 인용문으로 글을 시작하고, 긍정성, 정직, 신뢰라는 세 가지 핵심 요소를 제시한 점이 매우 인상 깊었습니다. 특히 `colossal`, `jeopardy`, `monotonous`와 같은 G6 수준의 고급 어휘를 사용하고, 책과 영화에서 가져온 다양한 예시를 활용하려는 모습에서 학생의 깊은 생각과 노력이 엿보였습니다.

에세이의 가장 큰 강점은 명확한 구조적 이해도와 풍부한 아이디어입니다. 서론에서 세 가지 핵심 가치를 명확히 제시하고, 각 본론에서 이를 설명하는 방식은 글의 논리성을 크게 높여주었습니다. 이는 'Advanced' 수준의 뛰어난 능력입니다.

한 단계 더 성장하기 위한 제안: 학생의 뛰어난 아이디어가 독자에게 더 잘 전달되려면 문법적 정확성을 높이는 것이 중요합니다.

  • 1. 주어-동사 수 일치: 주어가 단수인지 복수인지 확인하고 그에 맞는 동사를 사용하는 연습이 필요합니다. (예: `Helen Keller was` (O), `Hellen Keller were` (X))
  • 2. 동사의 올바른 시제 사용: 과거에 일어난 일에 대해 이야기할 때는 동사의 과거형을 정확하게 사용하는 것이 중요합니다. (예: `She persevered` (O), `She was persevere` (X))

학생은 이미 복잡한 주제에 대해 깊이 있게 생각하고 글의 뼈대를 세우는 훌륭한 능력을 갖추고 있습니다. 이제 그 뼈대 위에 문법적으로 정확한 문장이라는 살을 붙이는 연습을 꾸준히 한다면, 누구도 따라올 수 없는 멋진 글을 쓰게 될 거예요. 항상 응원하겠습니다!
