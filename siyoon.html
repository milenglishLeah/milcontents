The Person I Admire Most in History: King Sejong

A single individual can possess great power and influence the minds of many people. King Sejong is one of the greatest figures in Korean history who changed the way people think and live. As a celebrated king of the Chosun Dynasty, he is most remembered for his creation of Hunminjeongeum, the Korean alphabet. I admire King Sejong for two main reasons: his devotion to the common people and his advancements in science and technology.

First and foremost, I admire King Sejong for his dedication to serving the common people. His primary ambition was to create a better country for the underprivileged citizens of Chosun. To illustrate this, he invented the Korean alphabet so that commoners, who were previously illiterate, could finally learn to read and write. This incredible achievement shows that King Sejong was a truly compassionate leader who cared deeply for his people.

Secondly, King Sejong greatly advanced the fields of science and technology. He believed that alongside literacy, innovations in science were essential to improve the quality of life for everyone. For instance, he collaborated with the brilliant inventor Jang Yeong-sil to create revolutionary devices like the water clock and the sundial. Therefore, King Sejong's legacy in science and technology continues to connect with and influence us even today.

In conclusion, I admire King Sejong for his dedication to the common people and his use of scientific curiosity to improve their lives. By creating the Korean alphabet and promoting scientific inventions, he fundamentally improved his nation. I hope that I, too, can one day become a heroic figure who changes the world for the better, just like King Sejong.
